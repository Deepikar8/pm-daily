Operator of the day

Cat Wu on shipping speed

How Anthropic compresses six-month timelines to a week.

Interview  ·  Cat Wu, Anthropic  ·  April 26  ·  5 min

Cat Wu, Head of Product for Claude Code, argues the PM role is being rewritten in real time by AI-native engineering velocity. Six-month feature timelines have dropped to one week — sometimes one day. The traditional PM toolkit (multi-quarter roadmap alignment, partner-team coordination, detailed PRDs) is now the bottleneck, not the cure.

Her response is twofold: build concept corners — small product surfaces where one engineer can ship end-to-end weekly without coordination overhead — and hire engineers with strong product taste rather than additional PMs to do the speccing for them.

“We want to remove every single barrier to shipping things.”

The argument cuts across role definitions. “All the roles are merging,” Wu says. “PMs are doing some engineering work, engineers are doing PM work, designers are PMing and also landing code.” On her team, the highest-leverage hire isn't another PM — it's an engineer with product taste, capable of going from a piece of user feedback on Twitter to a shipped product by Friday with almost no PM involvement.

That has implications for what a PM should actually do all day. Wu's framing: the PM's question is no longer how to align quarterly roadmaps. It's how to maximize what today's model already does well, before it gets superseded.

Three takeaways

  1. 01Remove every barrier to shipping is the operating principle, not a slogan.
  2. 02The PM bottleneck is coordination overhead, not capacity.
  3. 03Develop product taste by using AI products yourself, every day.
