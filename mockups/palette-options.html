Decision frame

What are you optimizing for?

Editorial brand fit

If the canonical reference (warm paper, oxblood, Fraunces serif) is sacred, C (newspaper ink-blue) is the only option that diversifies *while* leaning further into editorial. Blue ink + warm paper is a real print archetype.

Pragmatic data UI separation

B (teal) gives the cleanest "brand vs data" channel split. Standard SaaS playbook. Accepts that PM Daily becomes more like Linear/Notion in feel, less like Stratechery.

Earthy warmth, no tonal shift

D (sage) matches paper/wood feel. Conflicts with our "correct = green" semantic; needs a downstream change to retire green-for-correct.

Stay-the-course minimum

E (expanded warm) adds more shades within warm. Cheapest change. Probably doesn't satisfy the reviewer's underlying point about the palette feeling narrow.

My read: C is the cleanest editorial answer. B is the pragmatic SaaS answer. Both are defensible; A and E aren't real fixes; D requires retiring green-for-correct first.
