## Pass 0 — Thesis brief
- Central tension: Doing brilliant PM work alone is enough → vs → Influence is itself the core PM skill; without it, even your best ideas die on the vine. PMs apply curiosity & empathy to users but drop those skills the moment they talk to an executive.
- Ideas (6):
  1. `influence-is-your-job` — "It's not my fault, but it is my problem." If leaders didn't buy in, that's your influence gap, not their bad judgment. [central]
  2. `exec-as-strobe-light` — Exec calendars are strobe lights; they haven't centered your problem; 30-second reset at top of meeting. [central]
  3. `apply-user-research-to-execs` — PMs use curiosity/empathy on users but forget those skills with execs; ask "what is the board pushing you on?" not "what's top of mind?" [central]
  4. `kill-things-build-trust` — Deprioritizing your own projects builds more trust than asking for more resources; signals aligned incentives. [central]
  5. `domain-expert-not-yes-man` — Influence ≠ agreement. Get paid to be the deepest person in the room with a customer anecdote in your back pocket. [secondary]
  6. `simulate-exec-with-ai` — Use AI to simulate execs: train on past product reviews / Slack to anticipate pushback before the meeting. [secondary]
