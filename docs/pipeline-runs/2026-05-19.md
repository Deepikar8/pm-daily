## Pass 0 — Thesis brief
- Central tension: "Most failed feedback isn't a recipient problem; it's a giver-framing problem. The standard PM-y move (lead with actions/impacts and conclude with vague 'circle back') consistently fails because it skips the shared Goal and the specific Next action — both of which determine whether feedback feels like a gift or a complaint."
- Ideas (5):
  1. `all-four-elements` — "Effective feedback must include all four GAIN elements (Goal, Actions, Impacts, Next actions); skipping any is the most common failure mode." [central]
  2. `framing-not-recipient` — "Repeat-feedback-without-change is data about the giver's framing, not the recipient's stubbornness." [central]
  3. `what-they-gain` — "Tell the recipient what *they* stand to gain — that flips defensiveness to receptivity." [central]
  4. `order-is-flexible` — "GAIN elements don't have to appear in order. Laura led with actions/impacts because the founder cared about those most in the moment." [secondary]
